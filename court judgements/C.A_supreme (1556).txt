IFTIKHAR MUHAMMAD CHAUDHRY, CJ
.- In purs uance of 
order dated 06.03.2013 vide notification dated 6.3.2013 M/s  2 
Anwar Ahmed Khan, SSP, Qamar Ahmed Sheikh, SDPO, Ali 
Azhar Iqbal,SI  have been suspended in view of the observations , 
which have been made therein. Similarly according to the 
statement of Mr. Anwar Mansoor Khan, l earned ASC for the 
Provincial Government , service of Mr. Fayyaz Ahmed Leghari, 
I.G.P. and Mr. Aleem Jafr i, D.I.G. have been surrendered to the 
Federal Government vide notification No. SOIII(S&GAD)POL -6-
VI/80 and No.SOIII(S&GAD)POL -6-IV/92 dated 6.3.2013.  
 
2. As far as our directions to the Chief Secretary, wh ich 
required him to look into the conduct of Mr. Shabbir Shaikh, 
DIG, presently holding the charge of IGP, as on the day of 
incident when he was holding the charge on behalf of Iqba l 
Mehmood, CCPO were complied with except informing the court 
in writing that he has entrusted the inquiry to some other police 
officers. We may point out that it was the personal obligation of 
the Chief Secretary to  inquire into the conduct of Mr. Shabbir  
Sheikh. The  information under the garb of the report submitted 
by him is not satisfactory. We therefore  direct hi m once again to 
comply with our  order in letter and spirit and submit  the report 
within 3 days to the Registrar for our perusal  in Chambers .  
 
3. No one has entered appearance on behalf of the Secretary 
Interior, Government of Pakistan, to submit report about the 
incident of Abbas Town  nor any report has been submitted by 
I.S.I and /or M.I. Process be repeated for calling the reports from 
the Secret ary Interior as well as the Secretary Defence 
communicating our o rder dated 06.03.2013 and the instant one  3 
to be transmitted to  the D.G. ISI and M.I. enabling them to put 
up the  report s in terms of the orders . The aforesaid reports 
should reach the Registr ar within three days from the date of 
communication of our orders, for our perusal in Chambers.   
 
4. On behalf of I.B. a report has been submitted by Mr. 
Ghulam Nabi Memon , Joint Director. Likewise  a report has also 
been placed on record by Mr. Shahid Hussain  Mahesar, S.P. 
Special Branch relating to the intelligence information. The D.G. 
Rangers has also filed a reply/ detailed report  along with the 
confidential memorandum. All the three functionaries i.e. I.B ., 
Special Branch and the Rangers to the extent of m emorandum 
filed by D.G. claimed confidentiality. Office is directed to seal all 
the three  reports  in separate envelopes and keep them  in safe 
custody.  
 
5. The Chief Secretary and Home Secretary were confronted 
with the aforesaid reports. We have reached the c onclusion that, 
prima facie , there is no coordination between the forces on 
intelligence sharing and concerted efforts are required to  
materialize such information between all these agencies. 
However , in order to fix responsibility  on any of institution, 
further material is awaited for examination without loosing sight 
of the national interest.  At this stage all these agencies named 
herein above besides ISI, MI and other agencies , responsible to 
generate information about the terrorism etc. targeting killing , 
and/or unrest  in the city , should cooperate and develop effective  4 
system so that they may succeed to achieve the object of 
ensur ing protection of  life and security of the general public.  
 
6. We have painfully note d that despite of our judgment in  
Watan Pa rty v. Federation of Pakistan (PLD 2011 SC 997)  no 
dedicated efforts had been made at all to depoliticize the police 
force and to assign task of controlling law &  order and 
preventing commission of terrorism and other crimes by 
appointing/posting honest, d edicated, committed and 
professional police officers. In  as much as despite of our 
observations the appointment and posting on contract basis , not 
only in the police department but in the Secretariat like Home 
Secretary, etc., had been maintained. In this behalf a list has 
also been produced before us by Mr. Ali Sher Jakhrani, AIG 
Legal. We have handed over copy of the same to the learned 
Advocat e General with direction to him to follow our earlier 
observation particularly in Paragraph Nos. 31, 32, and 33 i n the 
case of Watan Party and another vs. Federation of Pakistan and 
others  (PLD 2011 SC 9 97), relevant paras whereof are  
reproduced herein below.  
“31. It seems that the police primarily being responsible to 
enforce law and order has no intention to delive r. Either 
they are scared or they are dishonest or absolutely lack 
the requisite skills. It could be that in the year 1992 
operation clean up was launched against MQM wherein 
statedly, the police had played an active role, but 
subsequently, 92 police offic ers/officials disappeared and 
up till now there is no clue of their whereabouts nor is it 
known that by whom, and under whose patronage, such 
persons were abducted and /or killed. Another reason  5 
appears to be that police  force has been highly politicized, 
recruitments have been made on political considerations. 
It came to light during hearing of the case that in police 
force many police officers have been recruited  on political 
considerations who have managed to occupy such posts 
for extraneous consideration s and senior officers in the 
rank of SSP, SP and DSP etc. have been inducted  into the 
force from other organizations without following any rules 
and even they have not undergone training for the purpose 
of policing.  
…… …… ……  
 
32. The IGP stated that pres ently, the total strength of 
police force is 32524, out of which approximately 12000 
are performing security duties. Including 8000 deployed 
with VIPs and only 200000personnel of police force were 
left for the purpose of policing of 18 millions people in 
Karachi. He sated that by means of the Sindh (Repeal of 
the Police Order, 2002 and Revival of the Police Act, 1861) 
At, 2011 passed by the Provincial Assembly, the Police 
Order, 2002 has been repealed in the Sindh Province, as a 
result of which the Police A ct, 1861 has been revived and 
the IGP is helpless in transferring even a SDP from one 
place to another.  
 
33. The majority of the police force performing duty in 
Karachi comprises br ave and devoted officers and me n. 
They risk their lives often targeted by u nidentifiable 
enemies enjoying mobility in action. They deserve the 
respect of the nation. However, as the IGP conceded that 
30-40 percent of police force are non -cooperative either 
for the reasons that they have secured their appointments 
on political con siderations or they have associated 
themselves with different  groups including political parties , 
having vested interests in the affairs of Karachi, therefore, 
at times it becomes very difficult for him to effectively take 
action against the real culprits.  When called upon to 
explain as to how two accused persons involved in case  6 
FIR No.434 of 2011, Police Station Zaman Town, have 
been released by the Administra tive Judge, ATC, accepting 
their plea of alibi, where a concerted attack was launched 
upon police  officers/officials who were being transported in 
a private bus towards the disturbed areas for controlling 
riots and bringing peace, resulting in death of 6 -7 
policemen, causing injuries to others, the IGP disclosed 
that the investigating officer has been  suspended and the 
then SHO of the relevant Police Station has already been 
moved out and disciplinary proceedings are in hand 
against him. This categorical statement in respect of only 
one incident is sufficient to infer that the statement so 
made by the IGP carries sufficient weight. If the police 
official/officers are not sincere with the victims belonging 
to their own rank and file, what expectation could one 
have from such force, which, admittedly, stands 
politicized, for initiating forceful action aga inst the culprits 
involved in causing target killing, brutal murders, cutting 
limbs of human bodies and putting the dead bodies and 
torsos into bags with a view to terrorizing the society as a 
whole in general and the inhabitants of Karachi city in 
particu lar,. During the period of one month, i.e. from 24th 
July to 24t August 2011, as per information submitted 
before the Court about the killing of people of 306 persons 
232 FIRs have been registered mostly against unknown 
persons and on completion of investi gation in many cases, 
reports have been submitted declaring the cases for 
disposal under A class, which according to the relevant 
Police Rules means that the crime is untraceable.  
…… …..” 
 
7. We have been informed that about 631 cases are pending 
before the Anti Terrorism Courts  No.1, 2 and 3 of Karachi  and at 
the same time out of the cases , which have bee n proceeded so 
far, hundred per -cent acquittal has been recorded and 
unfortunately action against not a single Investigating officer has  7 
been initiated by the competent authority. The Prosecutor 
General  is directed to look into all these cases , wherein acquittal 
has been recorded and accused has been allowed to go scot -free 
and point out the defects in the investigation and initiate 
proceedings against those  Investigating officers  found 
responsible for the same  forthwith . In future all sincere efforts 
should be made by all the Investigating officers to collect 
incriminating evidence so the culprits , who are involved in 
putting  life and property of the citize ns in danger,  are brought to 
book. The ATC, though seems to be burdened with the cases , 
are not fulfilling with the requirements of Section 19(7) of Act 
1997, which mandates that such cases have to be decided within 
7 days, therefore, in view of the deterio rating law and order 
situation prevailing in Karachi, we direct the Provincial 
Government through Home Secretary that they should increase 
the number of A TC and the Registrar of the High Court is 
directed to place this order before the learned Chief Justic e to 
cooperate with the provincial government , instead of waiting for 
lengthy process of the recruitment of Specia l Judge  of the ATC 
on emergent basis , all the Sessions Judge s of Karachi  be 
confer red the powers of the ATC with direction to them that they 
should give preference , to dispose of these cases expeditiously , 
in view of deteriorating law and order of Karachi , which are 
required to be decided on urgent basis. While adopting this 
procedure, if need be, the Provincial Government may establish 
15 to 20  A.T.Courts in Karachi along with infrastructure , so that 
they may decide the cases on day to day basis as required by 
Anti Terrorism Act, 1997 and the prosecution if succeeds in  8 
producing the evidence and conviction is recorded, it would send 
message to t he like minded person . We know that our courts are 
already burdened with works but in order to meet with this 
special situation this exercise is , if taken for a period of at least 
three months , good  number of ATC cases will be disposed of  
particularly the cases of accused persons who are involved in the 
commission of the terrorism. Monitoring judge of the High Court 
and of this Court shall also watch proceedings of the ATC in 
terms of law i.e. Anti Terrorism Act, 1997, and the observations 
made in the case of Sheikh Liaquat Hussain .  
8. We have been informed that the present ATC are housed 
in dilapidated building . On getting such information we have 
obtained a report from the Registrar of High Court of Sindh and 
Incharge Branch Registry of this Court, contents whereof read as 
under:  
“It is respectfully submitted that in compliance of the 
above referred direction, the undersigned along with the 
Member Inspection Team of the High Court of Sindh and 
Mr. Mohammad Amin Khan, Officer In -charge Supreme 
Court, Branch Re gistry Karachi, visited the premises 
wherein, three Anti -Terrorists Courts of Karachi are 
functioning. The building is situated on Molvi Tameezuddin 
Khan Road in a Bungalow. The building is found in 
depleted condition. Inside the compound, the main 
buildin g is situated therein two courts are functioning on 
the ground floor and one court is functioning on first floor. 
The apartment of the building allocated for each court 
comprises three rooms amongst them one is comparatively 
larger where court room is made  while amongst the two

that the office of the Court Room No.1 is situated in a  9

lawyers could be arranged. It is noticed that at the time of 
the trial the Accused is hardly at a distance of seven to 
eight feet from the Presiding officer.

of the above stated specific directions we have visited the

4. It is further respectfully submitted that the Sindh 
Government has provided a space in Secretariat Barricks

5. The requisite report is submitted accordin gly.  
 
9. A perusal of the above report is self explanatory. It is also 
noteworthy that there is no arrangement of lock up inside the  10 
ATC building for the UTPs and they are confined in a cage , meant 
for the use of animals , which has no roof. This is violative  of 
Article 14 of the Constitution which gurantees the dignity of 
human being. We regret to observe that no one has checked up 
this position by physically visiting  the ATC Courts nor any 
responsible official has taken pain to see how  ATC are working . 
The Judges are sitting in  a place meant for  kitchen and/or in the  
small room s to conduct the Court proceedings . Copy of the  
report be sent to the Chief Secretary with direction to make 
immediate alternate /adequate arrangements for housing the 
courts along wit h lock ups where UTPs have to sit till their cases 
are taken up by the courts.  
10. Syed Hashim Raza Zaidi, Commissioner, Karachi appeared 
and filed statement informing the court that compensation to the 
families of deceased and the injured persons , is being pa id to 
them and such process of distributing the cheques has 
commenced , which is likely to complete during the course of the 
day. We have also asked him as to whether any alternate 
arrangement has been made for the residences of the p ersons 
who had lost the ir flats , etc. He stated that in the building 
situated in the vicinity , 32 flats have been acquired. He is 
directed to rehabilitate all those persons who were affected as  
during  the hearing we have watched a television program hosted 
by Mr. Hamid Mir , Anchor GEO TV  which showed the misries of 
the victims who had suffered during this incident on 3.3.2013. 
The DVD which was shown in the court room on the Multimedia , 
is directed to be made part of the record so that we may take 
assistance from it, if need be  while finally concluding this aspect  11 
of the case. Similarly, we have also asked to the Commissioner 
that he should take steps to provide subsistence allowance to 
the persons who had lost their business . He had promised to 
look into this aspect of the case  as well. In the meanwhile IGP is 
directed to constitute an investigating team of honest, dedicated 
and committed officers  headed by a senior police office  not 
below the rank of DIG to thoroughly conduct the investigation of 
the case. However, investigatio n team shall be free to co -opt 
professional police officers which would  extend the help in 
reaching the just and proper conclusion. The investigation shall 
be concluded  as early as possible but not more than the period 
prescribed under the law and challan against the culprits shall be 
submitted before the ATC. The ATC seized with the matter shall 
proceed with the case s and decided the same in terms of Section 
19 (7) of the Act and the observations made in the Sheikh 
Liaquat Husain’s  case. The IGP shall  subm it report in respect of 
proposed investigation investigation team within three days  to 
the Registrar for our perusal in Chambers . 
11. During the hearing , we have been informed that last night

the observations which have also been made by this court at 
times shall be complied with and a comprehensive report shall 
be submitted in this behalf on the next date of hearing.
